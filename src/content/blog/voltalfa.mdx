A lot of stuff has been going on lately. Getting to work long hours on a side project that had no shape, slowly becoming more than just a good intention, has finally started to show results. Back in October 2026, I was commisioned to refactor a retail POS software that had a lot of inconsistencies, most of them UI-wise, but after making a few frontend changes here and there, it slowly revealed a huge technical debt from its architecture and the database design (a lot of costly operations and tons of repeating code). With the aid of AI tools, I turned that flaky platform into a more robust and feature-rich application, one that the client was content with and wanted to see more features added to it. This was no longer just about refactoring a codebase, it was more about building a complete software with CRM capabilities, a database-driven storefront to help clients choose the product they dreamt of and an AI agent-based purchases recommendation system.

But what does it all have to do with VoltAlfa? Well, this software solution I worked on gave me the confidence to partner with a good friend (Rafa Salinas) to kickstart a startup – a small one, mind you – with the hopes of marketing a fully-featured POS, not only aimed at shoestores, but rather a tool that could be used by any retail store.

This is just the beginning.
